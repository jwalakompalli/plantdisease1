Treatment :

The management of peach bacterial spot involves a combination of cultural practices, sanitation measures, and the use of bactericides. Here are some treatment options:

Cultural practices: Good cultural practices can help prevent the spread of bacterial spot. These include pruning infected branches and twigs during the dormant season, removing and destroying infected leaves and fruit, avoiding overhead irrigation, and improving air circulation by spacing trees properly.

Sanitation measures: Practicing good sanitation measures is also important in managing bacterial spot. This includes cleaning pruning tools with a disinfectant between cuts, and removing any fallen leaves and fruit from the orchard floor.

Suggested Pesticide :

Some common pesticide used to treat peach bacterial spot include, Copper based fungicides and Kasugamycin.
