Treatment :

Apple black rot is a fungal disease that can affect apple trees, causing dark, sunken spots on the fruit and cankers on the branches. Here are some steps for apple black rot treatment:

Remove infected plant parts: Cut out and remove any branches, fruit, or leaves that are infected with black rot. This will help prevent the disease from spreading to other parts of the tree.

Apply fungicides: There are several fungicides available for apple black rot treatment, including copper-based fungicides and synthetic fungicides. Follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully for application rates and timing.

Suggested Pesticide :

Some common pesticidce used to treat apple black rot include, Captan, Dodine and Myclobutanil.
